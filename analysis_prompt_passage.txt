DIMENSION DEFINITIONS:

program_fit
  Does the student understand the program and have relevant motivation? Assess depth of explanation, specific knowledge of the program, and genuine connection to their goals. Generic "I like it" answers = weak. Specific, informed answers = strong.

job_readiness
  Does the student have a realistic and concrete career goal aligned with the program? Do they understand what role they are targeting and how the program leads there?

grit
  How did the student respond to adversity or difficulty questions? Do they show concrete strategies, persistence, and self-awareness? Vague answers like "I would manage stress" = weak. Specific plans = strong.

education_fit
  Does the student's stated academic background prepare them for this program? What subjects did they study, at what level, and how do they connect those to the program's requirements?

trustworthiness
  Are the student's answers internally consistent across the interview? Do they give direct answers to yes/no questions? Do they shift or contradict themselves? If answers change without explanation, mark weak.

financial_resources
  Are the student's funding sources clearly identified, realistic, and sufficient? Is there a coherent payment plan? Mark weak if amounts are vague, sources are unclear, or the plan has no backup.

network_support
  Does the student have family, community, or mentor support? Is that support confirmed and concrete (e.g., parents committed to specific amounts) or vague?

risk_level
  Synthesized from all other dimensions. Low = strong signals across most dimensions with minor gaps. Medium = mixed picture, some concerning gaps. High = multiple weak dimensions, inconsistencies, or unclear financial picture.

statement_of_purpose
  Does the student articulate a clear and compelling reason for pursuing this path? Do they communicate conviction and self-awareness? Not a written document assessment — assess from verbal responses only.

industry_experience
  Did the student mention any relevant work, projects, internships, or clubs in the target field? Concrete examples = strong. Vague mentions = medium. None mentioned = weak.

school_grades_quality
  Always set to not_assessed unless the student specifically mentions grades, GPA, or academic standing by name. This dimension requires document review.

---

RULES:

- Base all evidence strictly on what the student said in the transcript. Do not invent or infer.
- If a dimension cannot be assessed from the interview, set quality to not_assessed.
- Keep rationale concise, reviewer-facing, and grounded in transcript evidence.
- trustworthiness: If funding source answer shifts between turns without explanation, that is weak — note it explicitly.
- risk_level uses level (low/medium/high), not quality.
- reviewer_confidence: how much did the interview actually reveal? Low = many gaps and vague answers. High = direct answers to all key questions.
- Return valid JSON only.
